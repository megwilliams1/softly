## Future Features

### 🌧 1. The Mental Load Dump
A free-form brain dump space where moms can type everything at once — unstructured, unjudged.
Softly then gently sorts the dump into tasks (Sprouts), schedule items, and reminders.
- Free-text input, no formatting required
- AI or rule-based parsing into actionable categories
- Tone: calm, never overwhelming

### 🌱 2. Sprouts (Micro Tasks)
Tiny, low-friction tasks that don't belong on a calendar.
- Examples: sign permission slip, pack extra clothes, send $5 for pizza day
- Checked off = small bloom animation
- Daily engagement driver

### 🌼 3. Kid Profiles
Each child gets a profile — a plant in the Garden.
- Stores: clothes/shoe sizes, school info, allergies, activities, notes
- Connected across the whole app (activities, meals, reminders)
- Eliminates the "what size is she again?" problem

### 🌙 4. Evening Reset
A gentle end-of-day routine check-in.
- "Do you want to close the garden for today?"
- Shows what got done, what didn't (no guilt language)
- Tomorrow preview
- Ends with an affirmation: "You did enough today."

### 🌸 5. Care Circles
Private sharing with a partner, grandparent, or babysitter.
- View-only or collaborative access to schedules and tasks
- Reduces the "default parent" burden
- Not social media — intimate and intentional

### 🍯 6. Hearth → Smart Meal Assist
Level up the recipe section with meal intelligence.
- "What's in your fridge?" → meal suggestions
- "Busy night?" → quick recipe filter
- Auto-add ingredients to shopping/grocery list

### 🌿 7. Seasons (Emotional Patterns)
Soft behavioral insights framed as seasons, not analytics.
- "This week felt like a full garden."
- "You've been in a busy season."
- "You tend to need more rest midweek."
- Human, never robotic

### 🌷 8. The Plant (Deepen It)
Make the plant a living emotional anchor for the app.
- Grows when: tasks checked, journaling, showing up
- Struggles gently (no shame) during inactivity
- Blooming stages, seasonal changes, nameable
- Creates emotional attachment and daily return habit

---

### ⚠️ What NOT to Add
- Overly complex dashboards
- Productivity pressure features
- Anything that feels like "another responsibility"

Softly should always feel like "I want to open this" — never "I need to open this."
